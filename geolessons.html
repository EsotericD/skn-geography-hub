Weather & Climate

Study regional weather systems and hurricane formation in the Caribbean region.

CSEC Syllabus Summary

This topic examines the elements of weather (temperature, precipitation, humidity, etc.), weather instruments, factors affecting climate, and major climate types. Special focus on tropical weather systems including hurricanes, ITCZ, and anticyclones. Students should understand microclimates and the urban heat island effect.

Key Concepts:

  • Structure and formation of tropical cyclones
  • Factors influencing Caribbean climate (latitude, altitude, etc.)
  • Interpretation of climate graphs
  • Weather map analysis including synoptic charts

Tourism

How tourism shapes Caribbean economies, environments, and cultural landscape.

CSEC Syllabus Summary

Tourism is a leading economic sector in the Caribbean. Study includes types of tourism (eco, heritage, cruise, etc.), the multiplier effect, employment generation, and foreign exchange earnings. Also examines negative impacts like environmental degradation, cultural dilution, and economic leakage.

Key Areas:

  • Benefits and drawbacks of mass tourism
  • Sustainable tourism practices
  • Case studies of Caribbean tourism destinations
  • Government policies to manage tourism impacts

Natural Hazards

Learn about volcanoes, earthquakes, and disaster mitigation strategies.

CSEC Syllabus Summary

Focus on tectonic hazards (earthquakes, volcanoes) and climatic hazards (hurricanes, floods) affecting the Caribbean. Includes plate tectonics theory, monitoring techniques, and disaster preparedness. Case studies of recent Caribbean disasters required (e.g., Soufrière Hills eruption, Hurricane Maria).

Key Points:

  • Caribbean plate boundaries and associated hazards
  • Primary and secondary effects of hazards
  • Short-term and long-term responses
  • Community preparedness and CDEMA's role
